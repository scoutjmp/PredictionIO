Color of Night is a 1994 American erotic mystery thriller film produced by Cinergi Pictures and released in the United States by Hollywood Pictures. Directed by Richard Rush, the film stars Bruce Willis, Jane March, Ruben Blades, Lesley Ann Warren, and Scott Bakula. It is one of two well-known works by director Richard Rush, the other being The Stunt Man 14 years before. Although Color of Night flopped at the box office, this film did well in the home video market and became one of the Top 20 most-rented films in the United States in 1995. Maxim magazine also singled the film out as having the Best Sex Scenes in film history. Bill Capa (Bruce Willis), a New York City psychoanalyst, falls into a deep depression after a patient commits suicide in front of him by jumping from his office window. The sight of the bloody body of his patient clad in a bright green dress causes Capa to suffer from psychosomatic color blindness, taking away his ability to see the color red. To restart his life, Capa travels to Los Angeles to stay with a friend, fellow therapist and best-selling author Dr. Bob Moore, who invites him to sit in on a group therapy session.
