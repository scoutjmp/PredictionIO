Walking and Talking is a 1996 independent film starring Catherine Keener, Anne Heche, Todd Field, Liev Schreiber and Kevin Corrigan. Walking and Talking is a story about two women best friends and how they deal with their changing relationship as one (Heche) prepares to get married and the other (Keener) struggles with single life in New York City. Schreiber and Corrigan play love interests of Keener's while Field plays Heche's fiance. The film was written and directed by Nicole Holofcener. The film is #47 on Entertainment Weekly's "Top 50 Cult Films of All-Time" list. The film's soundtrack includes music from Billy Bragg, Yo La Tengo, Liz Phair, and The Sea and Cake. Walking and Talking has an overall approval rating of 86% on Rotten Tomatoes.
