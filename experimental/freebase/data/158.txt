Weekend at Bernie's is a 1989 comedy film directed by Ted Kotcheff and starring Andrew McCarthy and Jonathan Silverman as a couple of young insurance corporation employees who discover their boss is deceased. Believing that they are responsible for his death and that a hitman won't kill them if Bernie is around, they attempt to convince people that he is still alive. Larry Wilson and Richard Parker are two low-level employees at an insurance corporation in New York City, who uncover a $2 million fraud involving multiple life insurance policies that were issued after the death of the insured. Taking their findings to their boss Bernie Lomax, they are commended for discovering insurance fraud and invited to Bernie's "Hampton Island" beach house for the following Labor Day weekend. Unbeknownst to Larry and Richard, Bernie is behind the fraud and nervously arranges with his mob partners to have them both killed that weekend and arrange it as a murder-suicide. However, the gangsters double cross Bernie and decide to have him killed instead, citing his reckless greed and his affair with the mob boss Vito's girlfriend as reasons for his termination.
