Angel Baby is a 1995 Australian film written and directed by Michael Rymer, and starring John Lynch, Jacqueline McKenzie and Colin Friels. This film was taped in 1993-94. It documents the tale of two people with schizophrenia, Harry (Lynch) and Kate (McKenzie) who meet during therapy and fall passionately in love. They move in together and Kate gets pregnant. In the hospital, Kate dies while giving birth to their daughter. Harry asks his brother to take care of his daughter and walks to the bridge where Kate and Harry once stood to "fly". The film ends there and Harry's further actions are open for interpretation. The film swept the boards at the 1995 AFI Awards winning all the major categories as well as several major international film festivals. Angel Baby took $1,070,726 at the box office in Australia.
